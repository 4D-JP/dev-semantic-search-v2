WHY PREVIOUS FINE-TUNING FAILED — READ CAREFULLY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The model collapsed relevance levels 3, 2, and 1 to identical scores.
Two root causes:

ROOT CAUSE 1: Level 1 near-miss queries were topically related to the
passage but did not reuse the passage's primary legal terms. The model
learned domain similarity instead of concept-level discrimination.

Example of what caused the collapse:

  Passage: about "mandat exclusif vente pouvoirs signataire"
  Level 3: "mandat exclusif vente pouvoirs signataire"       ✓ correct
  Level 1: "clause pénale compromis vente immobilière"       ✗ WRONG
            ↑ same domain (real estate) but different terms
            ↑ model sees "vente immobilière" and scores it high
            ↑ cannot distinguish from level 3

  Correct level 1: "mandat exclusif distribution commerciale"
                    ↑ same primary terms (mandat exclusif)
                    ↑ completely different legal context (commercial)
                    ↑ model must learn the conceptual distinction

The failure mode in German:
  Passage: about "Verjährungsfrist Sachmängel 24 Monate"
  Level 1: "Garantie Kaufvertrag Sachmängel"                 ✗ WRONG
            ↑ same domain, different terms
  Correct level 1: "Verjährung Sachmängel arglistiges Verschweigen"
                    ↑ same primary terms
                    ↑ different legal regime

ROOT CAUSE 2: Level 3 queries were too generic, matching dozens of
passages across the corpus. This caused one query to be approved as
a valid positive for many unrelated passages, corrupting the model.

Example of what caused the collapse:
  Query: "Kündigung Arbeitsvertrag wichtiger Grund"
  ↑ matches every contract with a termination clause — dozens of passages
  ↑ model cannot learn which passage is the primary answer

  Query: "Haftung deliktisch leichte Fahrlässigkeit"
  ↑ matches every liability clause — hundreds of passages
  ↑ model collapses all liability passages to the same embedding

RELEVANCE LEVELS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

3 — HARD MATCH — THE PASSAGE IS THE ANSWER

The passage directly and specifically contains the clause, provision,
commitment, analysis, or decision being searched. A legal professional
would select this passage as the primary result.

Rules:
- Query must target the SPECIFIC legal mechanism in this passage —
  not the general topic that appears in many contracts
- The queried legal concept must be the PRIMARY subject of this
  passage — not one clause buried among many unrelated clauses
- For contract passages: query the specific obligation, condition,
  or right defined here — not the general clause type
- For email/memo passages: query the specific decision, instruction,
  or binding commitment made — not the general subject discussed
- For chunked passages: only query concepts that are substantively
  complete within this chunk
- If you cannot write a query that would NOT match dozens of other
  contracts, do not write a level 3 query — skip this passage

BAD level 3 (too broad — matches many passages):
  Passage: extraordinary termination clause
  BAD:  "außerordentliche Kündigung wichtiger Grund"
        ← appears in nearly every German contract
  GOOD: "außerordentliche Kündigung wiederholter Verstoß Abmahnung
         Bearbeitungsstandards"
        ← targets the specific trigger mechanism of THIS clause

BAD level 3 (too broad — matches many passages):
  Passage: liability limitation clause
  BAD:  "Haftung leichte Fahrlässigkeit Vertragspflicht"
        ← appears in nearly every German contract
  GOOD: "Haftung leichte Fahrlässigkeit Lizenzgeberin Sicherungsanzeige
         unbegründet"
        ← targets the specific liability context of THIS clause

BAD level 3 (email discusses, not contains):
  Passage: email saying "we should use the UNHCR report"
  BAD:  "humanitäre Klausel UNHCR-Bericht"
        ← email discusses it, does not contain it
  GOOD: only generate level 3 if passage contains the actual
        clause or substantive legal analysis, not a reference to it

2 — SOFT MATCH — RELATED BUT INCOMPLETE

The passage addresses the same legal concept but from a different
angle, covers a related sub-issue, or provides useful context without
being the direct answer. A legal professional would find it helpful
but continue searching.

Rules:
- Query targets an aspect the passage addresses only partially
- The passage is genuinely useful but not fully satisfying
- Must be clearly distinguishable from level 3 — not interchangeable

1 — NEAR MISS — SAME TERMS, DIFFERENT CONCEPT

CRITICAL LEVEL. This is where previous fine-tuning collapsed.

The query must reuse the primary legal terms from your level 3
queries but target a fundamentally different legal concept or context.
Surface similarity must be high; conceptual similarity must be low.

MANDATORY RULE:
Level 1 queries MUST contain at least one primary term from your
level 3 queries. If they do not share primary terms, the query
is in the wrong category — it is a level 0, not a level 1.

The level 1 query must place the shared term in a different legal
regime that changes its meaning entirely — not merely a different
sub-topic within the same legal domain.

Examples of correct near-miss construction:

  Level 3 term: "Kündigung" in an extraordinary termination clause
  Level 1 RIGHT: "Kündigung" in an employment dismissal context
                 ← same term, different legal regime entirely
  Level 1 WRONG: "Kündigung" in another contract termination context
                 ← same term, same legal regime — too similar

  Level 3 term: "Haftung" in a contractual liability clause
  Level 1 RIGHT: "Haftung" in a tortious liability context
                 ← same term, different legal basis
  Level 1 WRONG: "Haftung" in another contractual clause
                 ← same term, same legal basis — too similar

  Level 3 term: "mandat" in a real estate agency mandate
  Level 1 RIGHT: "mandat" in a corporate board mandate context
                 ← same term, different legal context entirely
  Level 1 WRONG: "mandat" in another agency context
                 ← same term, same legal context — too similar

0 — TRUE NEGATIVE — DIFFERENT AREA OF LAW

No meaningful overlap with the passage content or terminology.
Must come from a completely different area of law.

Rules:
  Commercial/contract law passage → criminal or family law query
  Administrative/public law passage → commercial or IP law query
  Asylum/immigration law passage → contract or corporate law query
  Real estate law passage → employment or criminal law query
  Employment law passage → real estate or corporate law query

Use plausible legal terminology — not random words.
Level 0 must never share primary terms with the passage.
